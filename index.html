About Me

I am a PhD student in the Department of Combinatorics and Optimization and the Institute for Quantum Computing at the University of Waterloo. I am supervised by David Jao and Michele Mosca.

My main research interests are cryptography and quantum information; particularly post-quantum cryptography and quantum cryptanalysis. My current work focuses on improving implementations of CSIDH by finding better parameter sets and faster algorithms for evaluating the class group action, and increasing its resilience against fault attacks.

Recreationally, I am interested in machine learning, data science, data visualization, and combinatorial design theory.
